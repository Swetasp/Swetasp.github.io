About Me

The Unexpected Journey from DNA to Code

My story starts in a biotechnology lab in India, where I was supposed to be analyzing genetic sequences. Instead, I found myself writing Python scripts to automate the tedious parts of my research. That's when I realized something: I wasn't just interested in understanding biological systems, I wanted to build the intelligent systems that could help us understand them better.

Fast forward to today, and I'm pursuing my Master's in Computer Engineering at the University of Florida, graduating May 2026. But here's what makes my path different: that biotechnology background isn't just a fun fact on my resume. It's my superpower. It taught me to think systematically about complex, interconnected problems, whether that's a metabolic pathway or a distributed computing architecture.

What Drives Me

I get excited about the intersection of AI and real-world impact. Not the "let's build another chatbot" kind of AI, but the kind that actually changes how people work and live. Like when I automated a genomics pipeline and watched weeks of manual analysis shrink to hours. Or when I built a healthcare platform that could intelligently match patients with the right specialists, considering everything from symptoms to insurance networks to urgent availability.

My Philosophy

I believe the best engineering happens when you deeply understand the problem you're solving. That's why I don't just build features, I build solutions. Whether I'm architecting cloud-native platforms, training deep learning models for medical imaging, or designing autoscaling systems for Kubernetes clusters, I'm always asking: "How does this create real value?"

I thrive in that sweet spot where machine learning meets production-ready code. Where research-grade accuracy meets enterprise-grade reliability. Where innovative ideas become scalable, maintainable systems that teams can actually use.

What's Next

I'm looking for opportunities where I can bring this blend of scientific thinking and engineering craftsmanship to challenging problems. Whether that's building the next generation of AI-powered healthcare tools, optimizing large-scale distributed systems, or creating platforms that make people's lives genuinely better, I want to be part of teams that are building things that matter.
